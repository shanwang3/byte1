How are people's feelings of happiness related to the geographic location and what factors contribute the feeling more?

The map visualizes the happiness scores of people in 158 countries collected through Gallup World Poll. People are asked to rate their own current lives on the scale from 0 to 10 (the best possible life for them being a 10 and the worst possible life being a 0). We can see that people in European countries generally have higher scores those in the African area have lower scores.

Using the same data set, the chart below displays the happiness scores of people in 100 countries with the extend that the country's GDP and sense of freedom contribute to their happinese level. The comparison shows that for most countries, the economy contributes more to their sense of happiness, especially for those with high happiness scores.
